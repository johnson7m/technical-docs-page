What is Synergy?

Synergy can be thought of as the cumulative mechanical/aesthetic interactions (positive or negative) between individual cards in and outside of a play structure.

In ther words, two cards have very little synergy if they have no mechanical/aesthetic relevance to one another.

Interactions can be thought of as the intended or unintended effect of a card once it is relevant to a board state (ie. in your hand, battlefield, library, exile, etc)

In essence, the general process for understanding the overall synergy of a card goes like this:

  1. Evaluate the card's general power level when compared to other, similar cards (similar cmcs, types, subtypes, color, etc).

  2. From there identify the most interactive or strongest elements of the card and if they can be leveraged in a particular card strategy. For example, large creatue cards that can discard themselves like [[Troll of Kahza'dun]] are naturally more effective in strategies that bring creatures back from the graveyard (take Legacy Reanimator or Scam decks for example)

  3. After doing some research either from your experience and own knowledge of other cards or external sources such as Scryfall. Sort the card into three synergy categories: High, Medium, Low.

In essence, that's all there is to synergy. Of course there are definitely more detailed and precise ways to factor a cards overall synergy and power-level, but for those just beginning to build their own decks, this methodology will go far in besting your local play group.
